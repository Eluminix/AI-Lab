    LOS ANGELES >> Attack early and attack often.

    The Sierra Canyon girls basketball team executed its game plan to perfection in its Southern California Regional Open Division opener at rival Windward.

    Then Windward staged a late insurgence that left Sierra Canyon stunned in a whirlwind final five minutes for a 65-59 victory Wednesday night at Windward School, advancing to Saturday’s regional semifinals against Long Beach Poly.

    With a fantastic and raucous girls basketball crowd, Windward (26-5) thrived down the stretch, making 11 of 12 free throws to overcome their Gold Coast League rivals and avenge a wild 56-54 loss on Jan. 22 that gave Sierra Canyon the league title.

    It was another lively chapter in the Sierra Canyon-Windward legacy.

    “We love it. We love the noise,” Windward’s Juju Watkins said. “We were thinking we were not going to give this up. We worked so hard and we’ve been through so much.”

    Watkins, just a freshman, scored 12 of her 20 points in the fourth quarter, coming up clutch when Windward needed her most.

    “She has big-game blood,” Windward coach Vanessa Nygaard said. “She’s learning a lot. It’s easy to forget that she is still just a freshman and she makes errors, but she has got something special.”

    Sierra Canyon (25-7) led 57-47 with 5:20 left in the game, but Windward took its first lead 58-57 on two free throws by Watkins with 3:07 left.

    McKayla Williams added 16 points for Windard, and Maya Stokes came off the bench to contribute a 12-point performance.

    “We showed our heart,” Williams said. “We lost by two earlier in the year, and we really wanted it. We were in a huddle late in the third quarter, and we realized we could hit that switch. One stop at a time.”

    Sierra Canyon coach Alicia Komaki praised her team for its performance and execution.

    “Everyone who played should have no regrets,” Komaki said. “They followed the game plan. The defense was fantastic. We always play hard and we always play with passion and always do things the right way. Our seniors played to win and we left it all on the floor.

    “That’s why we’re so confused. When we play really well like this, we always end up on the right side of the win column. We didn’t tonight.”

    Windward went to a triangle-and-two to slow down Ashley Chevalier and Vanessa DeJesus of Sierra Canyon.

    Chevalier scored 10 first-quarter points and had 21 points at halftime, but she was limited to four second-half points.

    Still, the reigning Daily News Player of the Year delivered one of her best performances of the season, pushing the pace and giving her team an early surge in her 25-point performance.

    “It was my last game as a senior, and I was going to do everything I could to help the team,” Chevalier said. “It felt like I finally came into myself. This felt like me for the first time in a long time.”

    Alexis Mark scored 11 points for Sierra Canyon. DeJesus scored seven points, and Vanessa Smart had six points for Sierra Canyon.
