    Marvel teases all the first big MCU shows at the Super Bowl

    Falcon and the Winter Soldier, Wandavision, and Loki all get a moment

    by Petrana Radulovic

    Feb 3, 2020, 2:22 AM UTC
    Petrana Radulovic is an entertainment reporter specializing in animation, fandom culture, theme parks, Disney, and young adult fantasy franchises.

    Our first look at The Falcon and the Winter Soldier, Wandavision, and Loki, the first Disney Plus entries into the Marvel Cinematic Universe, is finally here.

    Premiering during Super Bowl 54, the tease reintroduces Sam Wilson (Anthony Mackie) and Bucky Barnes (Sebastian Stan) following the events of Avengers: Endgame. Plot details have been kept pretty tight, but after the events of Endgame — wherein Steve Rogers hands the shield and the Captain America mantle to Sam — many wondered about what that meant for this series. Is something stopping Sam from becoming Captain America? He can certainly throw the shield.

    Captain America: The Winter Soldier actress Emily VanCamp returns for Falcon and the Winter Soldier as SHIELD Agent Sharon Carter, while Wyatt Russell makes his MCU debut as John Walker, known to comics fans as U.S. Agent. Daniel Bruh joins as Civil War foe Zemo.

    After the wave of action-filled footage, we get a small snippet of footage from Wandavision, finding Vision and Scarlet Witch back in a reality-bending drama. The couple appear to be jumping from sitcom to sitcom — Wanda gasps in the Brady Bunch house? Wanda gasps in the Brady Bunch house.

    We also short line-reading from Tom Hiddleston as his Thor villain Loki, appearing in a prison outfit. Let the speculation begin.

    Unlike the Hulu and Netflix Marvel shows, the Disney Plus ones will actually affect the events of the movies. The Falcon and the Winter Soldier heads to Disney Plus this fall, with WandaVision following later this year.
