CSUF women’s basketball begins road trip

  • By Trevor Vernola and Keith Krikorian
  • Feb 12, 2020
  • Feb 12, 2020

The CSUF women’s basketball team will head into enemy territory today as they travel to UC Irvine for a Big West matchup at 7 p.m.

The team will attempt to build off the momentum from their 71-61 victory over UC Riverside on Saturday evening. UC Irvine is also coming off a recent victory as they took down first place UC Davis in a 73-68 win. 

The Titans currently sit in fourth place in the Big West Conference, with an overall record of 12-9 and a conference record of 4-4. UC Irvine is currently in a three-way tie for last and has an overall record of 7-16, with a 3-6 record in conference play.

Fullerton prioritized their defense in the first quarter of their game against UC Riverside, which allowed them to hold a lead the entire game. Head coach Jeff Harada said the team was focused on defending early on to avoid having to come from behind later in the game.

“It’s really important we had to set the tone early with our defense. We wanted to get up and down in our transition offense. We wanted to get them down and make them play from behind. We just need to set the tone and get a quick start,” Harada said.

The Titans intend to keep the same mentality in their upcoming game against Irvine.

CSUF players to look out for tonight include Taylor Turney and Raina Perez, who scored a combined 43 points in their most recent game against Riverside. Freshman forward Megan Nieto came into her own last game, netting a career-high 13 points, so she could play a strong role coming off the bench.

Fullerton is the best-scoring team in the conference, averaging 69.6 points a game, which is 2.5 points higher than Big West conference leader UC Davis. 

Last season, UC Irvine limited Perez to 27 total points over two games. If Irvine continues to successfully limit Perez’s shooting opportunities this season, look for an increase in her 86 total assists. 

CSUF continued their efficient shooting in the last game, outshooting their overall clip of 44.2%, which leads the Big West Conference. 

UC Irvine has the second-worst rebound margin per game at -6.9. CSUF could take 6-foot-2-inch sophomore Madison Freemon and 6-foot-3-inch sophomore Janette Mensah off of the bench to expose UC Irvine’s weakness.

Fullerton will need to lock down Irvine’s senior guard and top scorer, Lauren Saiki. Saiki is coming off of four consecutive appearances in which she has scored at least 14 points.

Today’s meeting will be the first time the two Big West teams face off this season. 

UC Irvine will make their way to the Titan Gym on March 7 for CSUF’s final game of the regular season.
