Quinata Roo suspends alcohol sale for 14 days due to Covid-19 details below

March 27, 2020

    Chetumal.- “In order to maintain public order in Quintana Roo and due to the health situation of the Coronavirus, the sale of alcoholic beverages is temporarily suspended at extraordinary times indicated in Article 25 bis of said Law”, the Twitter posted by the Ministry of Finance and Planning (Sefiplan) of Quintana Roo that directs all those who sell intoxicating drinks in the entity.

    The “notice” is “to all owners and / or managers of establishments where alcoholic beverages are sold throughout the territory of the state of Quintana Roo,” who are informed of the temporary suspension of the sale during extraordinary hours in Bacalar, Benito Juárez, Cozumel, Felipe Carrillo Puerto, Isla Mujeres, José María Morelos, Lázaro Cárdenas, Othón P. Blanco, Puerto Morelos, Solidaridad and Tulum.

    According to the notice, it will be “from 23:00 on the 27th of March 2020 and will be in force until 23:00 on the 10th of April 2020

    According to article 25 Bis of the “Law on the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages in the state of Quintana Roo”, the extraordinary schedule to which it refers is “from 23:00 to 03:00 the next day, from Monday to Sunday ”, which is suspended for 14 days.

    Source: poresto.net, quintafuerza.mx
